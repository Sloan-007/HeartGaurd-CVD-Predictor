F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Your concerns about heart health are important to us. Explore our FAQs for insights into cardiovascular health and prevention.

  • What are the major risk factors for cardiovascular disease?

    Major risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, poor diet, physical inactivity, and excessive alcohol use.

  • What symptoms may indicate a cardiovascular problem?

    Symptoms can vary but often include chest pain, shortness of breath, palpitations, dizziness, fainting, and swelling in the legs due to fluid buildup.

  • Are there early warning signs of heart disease?

    Early signs can be subtle, like occasional chest discomfort, fatigue, indigestion, or shortness of breath. It's crucial to consult a healthcare provider for any concerns.

  • Can cardiovascular disease be cured?

    Some forms of heart disease can be cured or managed effectively through lifestyle changes, medications, or surgical procedures. Early detection and treatment are key.

  • How do diet and exercise affect heart health?

    A diet low in saturated fats, trans fats, and cholesterol, with a focus on fruits, vegetables, and whole grains, can help reduce heart disease risk. Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy weight, lower cholesterol, and reduce blood pressure.
